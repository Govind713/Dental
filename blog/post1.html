How to prepare for a root canal — what to expect

A short guide to help you feel prepared and comfortable before your appointment.

Before the appointment

  • Eat a light meal unless instructed otherwise.
  • Bring a list of medications and medical history.
  • Arrange transportation if you plan sedation.

During the procedure

Local anesthesia is used; you should feel numb but not pain. The dentist removes infected tissue, cleans and shapes the canals, and places a temporary or permanent filling.

Aftercare

Expect some tenderness for a few days; over-the-counter pain relievers usually manage discomfort. Keep follow-up appointments for final restoration.
